Design and Mechanics

A balisong is made of three main parts: a blade, two handles that rotate around the tang, and a latch to hold it closed. The knife’s unique folding design allows the blade to be hidden safely inside the handles when not in use, making it both compact and secure.

The handles are usually made of materials like stainless steel, titanium, or G10 fiberglass, while the blade is often made from carbon steel or Damascus steel for strength and edge retention. The smooth rotation is achieved by using either washers or bushings, which reduce friction and make flipping easier.

Many balisongs today are designed for flipping practice rather than cutting. These versions, called trainers, have blunt edges so users can safely learn tricks such as the “basic open,” “aerial,” or “behind-the-back” flip without injury.

The balance between the blade and handles is very important. A well-balanced balisong feels light in hand and allows smooth transitions between moves. Enthusiasts often customize their knives by adjusting handle weights or swapping pivot screws to achieve the perfect feel.

Overall, the design of the balisong shows how engineering and art can come together: it’s a practical tool, a performance instrument, and a symbol of precision craftsmanship.
